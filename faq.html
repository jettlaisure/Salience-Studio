SEO & AEO

What is AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)?
AEO is the practice of optimising content to be cited by AI tools like Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity. It involves structuring content so AI systems can extract and attribute it as a direct answer to a user's question – placing the brand in AI-generated results pages alongside or above traditional search listings.
How is AEO different from SEO?
SEO focuses on ranking in traditional search results. AEO focuses on being cited as a source by AI systems that generate answers to user queries. Both matter – users are now split between clicking search results and reading AI-generated summaries. A complete search strategy covers both.
How long does SEO take to show results?
SEO typically shows meaningful results in 3–6 months. Technical fixes and on-page changes can improve rankings within weeks. Content and authority-building takes longer – most clients see significant organic growth between months 4 and 9.
What's the difference between SEO and paid ads?
Paid ads generate traffic immediately but stop the moment you stop paying. SEO builds organic search presence that compounds over time – once you rank, traffic is effectively free. Most product brands benefit from both, but SEO provides better long-term ROI for brands with strong content and clear positioning.
How do you optimise for Google AI Overviews?
We optimise for Google AI Overviews by structuring content with direct, specific answers to real questions, implementing FAQPage and other schema markup, building topical authority through keyword clusters, and ensuring content is crawlable and well-linked. AI Overviews tend to cite sources that answer questions clearly and authoritatively.
How do you optimise for ChatGPT and Perplexity?
ChatGPT and Perplexity draw from indexed web content. We optimise for these by building topical authority – lots of high-quality, well-structured content in the brand's subject area – earning backlinks from credible publications, and ensuring the brand's name and expertise appear consistently across the web.
What is a topic cluster and why does it matter?
A topic cluster is a group of related content pages built around a central pillar page – all internally linked. Search engines use clusters to assess topical authority: a site with deep coverage of a topic ranks higher than one with thin, scattered content. We build clusters for every SEO client to establish authority in their most important search areas.

Brand PR

What is digital PR and how does it help my brand?
Digital PR secures editorial coverage and influencer placements in online publications and social channels. It builds credibility with the right audiences, generates high-quality backlinks that strengthen SEO, and creates the kind of third-party validation that paid ads can't replicate.
What's the difference between PR and influencer marketing?
Editorial PR targets publications and journalists – the result is an article or feature that lives permanently on the publication's site and drives long-term SEO value. Influencer marketing targets creators with an audience – the result is social content with immediate reach. Both are part of our brand PR service and work best together.
How do you measure PR results?
We measure PR through placements secured (publication name, domain authority, reach), backlinks generated, estimated referral traffic, and brand mention volume over time. We provide a monthly placement summary with these metrics.
How long before we see press coverage?
First placements typically appear within 4–8 weeks of campaign launch. Editorial lead times vary – some publications move in days, others take 6–8 weeks to publish after a pitch is accepted. We set realistic expectations based on the outlets we're targeting.
What kind of publications can you get coverage in?
We target online publications appropriate to the brand's niche, audience, and positioning. Coverage is earned, not paid – we pitch on merit and target outlets that your customers actually trust and read.

Email & SMS

What email platforms do you work with?
We work primarily with Klaviyo for e-commerce brands, as it integrates directly with Shopify and has the strongest segmentation and flow capabilities for product launches. We also work with Mailchimp and other platforms if the client already has an established setup.
How many emails per month is typical?
Most retainers include 2–4 campaign emails per month plus active automated flows. The right cadence depends on launch frequency and list size – we calibrate to avoid list fatigue while staying present enough to drive revenue.
What open rates can I expect?
E-commerce brands on our retainer typically see open rates of 35–55% for campaign emails and 45–65% for automated flows. Results depend on list health, segmentation, and subject line quality – all of which we manage.
Is SMS marketing legal and effective?
Yes, SMS marketing is legal with proper consent – subscribers must opt in explicitly, and every message must include an opt-out option. It's also highly effective for product launches: SMS typically sees open rates above 90% and click rates 5–10× higher than email, making it ideal for time-sensitive announcements.
